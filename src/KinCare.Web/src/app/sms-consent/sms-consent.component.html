SMS Opt-In & Consent Process

Last updated: {{ lastUpdated }}

This page documents, in full, how KinCare obtains consent from transport vendors ("drivers") before enrolling them to receive SMS messages through the Service. It exists specifically so that our messaging opt-in process is publicly verifiable, in addition to being described in our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

Who opts in, and how

The recipients of KinCare's SMS messages are transport vendors — drivers or transport companies contracted by a senior living facility to transport residents. KinCare does not send marketing or promotional SMS to consumers, residents, or the general public.

Vendors are not self-service sign-ups. There is no public web form or SMS keyword a stranger can use to start receiving KinCare messages. Instead, opt-in happens as part of a real, individual business relationship: a facility coordinator brings on a specific, named transport vendor as an approved service provider, and obtains that vendor's explicit consent verbally — by phone or in person — before entering their phone number into the KinCare system.

The exact consent script used

Before a vendor's phone number is ever entered into KinCare, the onboarding coordinator reads them the following, in full, and requires an explicit "yes":

"As a KinCare transport partner, you'll receive text messages from KinCare for ride dispatch requests and status updates — including new pickup requests, and status check-in prompts as you complete each trip. Message frequency varies with how many rides you're assigned to. Message and data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help, or STOP at any time to opt out of these messages. Our Terms of Service are at kincare-web.onrender.com/terms and our Privacy Policy is at kincare-web.onrender.com/privacy. Do you consent to receive these text messages — yes or no?"

Only after the vendor affirmatively answers "yes" does the coordinator proceed to enter the vendor's name and phone number into KinCare. This satisfies each required element of a compliant opt-in disclosure: service description, message content, message frequency, the rates disclaimer, HELP instructions, STOP/opt-out instructions, links to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy, and an explicit request for consent.

Confirmation of enrollment

Once a vendor's number is entered following the consent script above, they are enrolled and begin receiving ride dispatch and status SMS messages tied to residents they've been engaged to transport, sent from KinCare's registered number. They may reply STOP at any time to opt out — this is handled automatically by our messaging provider (Twilio)'s built-in opt-out handling, independent of KinCare's own systems, ensuring opt-out requests are always honored even in the event of an application outage.

No text-based ("keyword") opt-in is offered

KinCare does not support opting in by texting a keyword to our number — there is no public "text JOIN to sign up" flow, because our recipients are known, individually contracted business vendors, not the general public. Any message received from a number not already onboarded through the process above is not enrolled to receive further messages.

Message content and frequency

Vendors receive messages tied to ride dispatch and status updates — new ride offers, one-tap status check-in prompts, and trip-completion confirmations. Message frequency varies directly with how many rides a vendor is assigned; a vendor assigned no rides in a given period receives no messages. Message and data rates may apply.
